MASHED POTATO RECIPE!!

Follow along!

Mashed potatoes is a classic and popular dish made by boiling potatoes until they are tender and then mashing them to a smooth and creamy consistency. Butter, milk, or cream is usually added during the mashing process to enhance flavor and texture. The dish is seasoned with salt and pepper, and sometimes other herbs or spices can be included for added taste. Mashed potatoes can be served as a side dish alongside various meats, vegetables, or gravy, and it is loved for its comforting and satisfying nature.

THIS IS WHAT YOU'LL NEED!

INGREDIENTS

  • Potatoes
  • Butter
  • Milk or cream
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Optional: Herbs or spices for seasoning (e.g., garlic, chives, parsley)

GETTING YOUR HANDS DIRTY!

THAT'S HOW YOU DO IT!

  1. Peel and chop the potatoes into evenly sized chunks.
  2. Rinse the chopped potatoes under cold water to remove excess starch.
  3. Place the potatoes in a large pot and cover them with cold water.
  4. Bring the water to a boil over high heat and add a pinch of salt to the water.
  5. Reduce the heat to medium-low and let the potatoes simmer for about 15-20 minutes or until they are fork-tender.
  6. While the potatoes are cooking, prepare the other ingredients.
  7. Drain the cooked potatoes in a colander and return them to the pot.
  8. Add the butter to the hot potatoes.
  9. Mash the potatoes and butter together using a potato masher or a fork until they are mostly smooth.
  10. Gradually pour in the milk or cream while continuing to mash until you achieve your desired creamy consistency.
  11. Season the mashed potatoes with salt and pepper to taste.
  12. If desired, add any additional herbs or spices for extra flavor.
  13. Give the mashed potatoes a final stir to incorporate all the ingredients evenly.
  14. Serve the delicious mashed potatoes hot as a delightful side dish!
